Creamy Bay Scallop Spaghetti

Fresh creamy Bay Scallop Spaghetti


This scallop pasta dinner with a creamy sauce is fast and easy. I cook the
scallops a bit longer than many chefs, but that gives the dish more flavor
and they're still tender and moist. I think you'll agree, the sherry sauce benefits
significantly from the longer cook time.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces uncooked thick spaghetti
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 pound bay scallops
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼2 teaspoons grated lemon zest
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes
  • ⅓ cup dry sherry
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, for serving

Steps

  1. Step 1
    Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling
    water, stirring occasionally until tender yet firm to the bite, about 10 minutes
    or 1 minute less than directed on the package.
  2. Step 2
    Meanwhile, heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. When oil just starts to
    smoke, add scallops and move them into a single layer. Let sear on high for
    about 1 minute. Toss to turn.
  3. Step 3
    Pour in cream. When mixture begins to simmer, reduce heat to medium-low.
    Add salt, pepper, and lemon juice.
  4. Step 4
    Drain pasta. Transfer to skillet with scallops; bring to a simmer. Add 1/2 of the
    chopped parsley. Cook until pasta is heated through and tender, about 1
    minute.
